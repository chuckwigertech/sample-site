Senator Chuck Wiger

I grew up in North St. Paul. So did my parents, George and Helen. I graduated from North High, Hamline University (JD, BA) and the University of Colorado, Boulder, Institute for Organization Management.

I’m an attorney and recently retired from Securian (law/compliance). I previously worked in government affairs for the St. Paul Area Chamber of Commerce, and also managed several east metro community chambers. Prior to that, I had a variety of very interesting jobs: dishwasher, custodian, parked cars, waiter, traveled with the circus, clubhouse assistant for Doc Lenz and Ray Crump/Minnesota Twins, umpire, reporter, orderly, deckhand on ship and lobbyist.

I have been actively involved in public service and community for most of my adult life. I was first elected to the District 622 North Saint Paul-Maplewood-Oakdale School Board when I was 21. For three decades, I was elected and re-elected seven times and completed my final three years as chair. I was also elected twice as a Supervisor on the Ramsey County Soil and Water Conservation District Board. From 1983 to 1989, I served as a member of the Metropolitan Council representing suburban Ramsey, Washington, and Hennepin counties.

As a state senator, I chaired the education policy and finance committees, and the election committee, plus the senate legislative delegations for Ramsey and Washington Counties, and St. Paul. I work hard and thoughtfully. I have earned numerous Legislator of the Year awards and recognition from groups representing our schools, the environment, social justice, labor, business and health care.

I live in Maplewood and have five children: Jessica, Charlie, Angelina, Brittany and Carolyn and five grandchildren: Mason, Avery, Zachary, Adrianna and Luca. I coached youth sports for over 25 years, served on the parish council and numerous community boards and committees. In my spare time, I enjoy family, playing scrabble and the outdoors.
