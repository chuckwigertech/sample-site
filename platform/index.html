Priorities: Building a Brighter Future🌈

We face many challenges. ﻿Top priority for our state and nation is to navigate and responsibly attack COVID-19. Successfully addressing the pandemic will have the biggest impact regarding jobs and our economy. In 2021 the legislature needs to adopt a balanced two-year budget. Among the key issues: education opportunity, access to affordable health care, environmental protection and climate change, and equity for all.

For updates on these and other issues, townhall meetings, plus initiatives I am sponsoring or co-authoring, please sign up to get my e-newsletter. Your questions, input and guidance are much appreciated!

COVID-19: Coping, Recovering, Reopening

The pandemic has disrupted everyone’s life. As we continue to respond to the challenges presented by COVID-19, we need to listen to the experts, follow science and act responsibly to protect our community. Effectively managing the virus is the best way to help our schools and businesses so our economy can come back faster and stronger.

I support bipartisan efforts to provide needed support for hospitals, first responders, front line workers, education, business, persons unemployed or needing a safety net, and our communities. I will continue to put people first, and ensure Minnesotans remain safe and healthy and get the care they deserve.

Education: Students are Our Future

Education opportunity for each child is key to their future success. Our economy and democracy thrives on quality schools. That’s why our state constitution underscores the importance of education and the largest part of our state budget invests in schools.

The pandemic has significantly impacted students, families and school staff. We need to further review this and provide needed resources. Increased demand for mental health services, counseling and protection equipment are among the needs. Federal assistance is needed, too.

Affordable postsecondary education is also a high priority. I’m very proud to have Century College in my district. I work closely with the school to encourage and provide opportunities. Northeast Metro 916 has many career and technical programs plus other specialized training. Labor-business apprentice training programs continue to grow and provide great opportunities. Whether it be the University of Minnesota, our state university or private college programs, or military career training, opportunities abound to train or retrain people for our workforce. Our economic future is directly linked to the success in these various programs and opportunities.

Environment: preserve, protect

Clean water and air should be available for everyone, everywhere. We must increase clean energy and combat the effects of climate change. It’s crucial to protect our families and environment from harmful chemicals. Minnesota is the first state to ban TCE (trichloroethylene). I was the Senate chief author of the proposal recommended by the state’s Pollution Control Agency.

It’s crucial to protect our families and environment from harmful chemicals. Minnesota is the first state to ban TCE (trichloroethylene). I was the Senate chief author of the proposal recommended by the state’s Pollution Control Agency.

When the State of Minnesota settled its lawsuit against 3M for contaminating our drinking water with chemicals, I advocated that funds be provided to our communities to safeguard access to clean, safe drinking water. The settlement funds will exclusively fund drinking water and natural resource projects in East Metro communities affected by the contamination.

Climate pollution creates inequities in our communities of color and under-resourced communities. I support requiring companies to notify residents and consumers of environmental hazards, removing harmful carcinogens and toxins from our food packaging and furniture, and requiring industrial facililities to comply with clean water standards.

I’m proud to be a part of the Senate DFL’s Clean Energy and Climate Caucus. Investing in clean energy and green infrastructure helps us be good stewards of our planet and creates great jobs.

Health Care: access, affordable

Affordable, accessible medical and dental care is fundamental. An accident or unexpected illness shouldn’t put any family’s financial security at risk. No one should be denied insurance coverage if they have a pre-existing condition.

I will continue to advocate proposals addressing health care access and affordability, reducing the cost of prescription drugs and increasing telemedicine options. These measures are vital to the health and prosperity of our families and communities. As our area’s former state senator and senate president, Jerry Hughes, use to say, “your health is your wealth.”

Equity for all

Everyone deserves fair treatment and equal access to opportunity. As a legislator, and former school board member, I have always advocated these fundamental principles. The song, We Are All God’s Children, reflects some of my beliefs, and if you’re interested:

All of us can contribute by being respectful and kind to one another. As legislators, we need to continue addressing equality and equity in our policies and budget. I will work with many groups on this challenge and opportunity for us to do better. Working together and achieving bipartisan cooperation, especially now, is very important for us to make meaningful progress. I will do all I can.
